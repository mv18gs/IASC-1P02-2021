What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. This can be calculated by dividing weight (kg) by the square of height (m). A BMI of less than 18.5 indicates underweight, 18.5 to 24.9 indicates normal weight, 25 to 29.9 indicates overweight, and more than 30.0 indicates obesity. This measurement is important to generally predict health risks or problems. However, it does not indicate the amount or pattern of fat and lean mass, nor the differences in activity level. For example, a high BMI does can either mean that a person is obese or high in muscle mass. This is also one of the limitations from this measurement such that it is not appropriate for those who are high in muscle mass, pregnant or nursing, under 18 years old, and more.
